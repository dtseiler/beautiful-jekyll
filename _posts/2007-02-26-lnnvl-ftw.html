--- layout: post title: lnnvl() ftw date: '2007-02-26T19:02:00.000-06:00' author: Don Seiler tags: - 'null' - oracle - lnnvl modified_time: '2010-11-19T22:48:47.479-06:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-7032512792942232766.post-2218595970420689413 blogger_orig_url: http://www.seiler.us/2007/02/lnnvl-ftw.html --- Courtesy of Jonathan Lewis, I present you with LNNVL(). For those of you who forget to consider the possibility of NULL values in your query predicates, this could be a godsend. Should clean up a lot of predicates that had to use OR cases just to separately check for NULLs.

As Jonathan describes it: "The function lnnvl(predicate) returns true if its parameter returns false or null."
